Frequently Asked Questions

What is BrowsePad? +

BrowsePad is an Android TV browser built to redefine how browsing works on TV by centering the experience around BrowsePad Connect and phone-powered trackpad control.

How do I browse if a normal TV remote feels limited? +

BrowsePad includes both D-pad browsing and an app-wide pointer, but its main advantage is BrowsePad Connect, which lets your phone take over as a trackpad-style remote for smoother browsing.

Can I open multiple tabs? +

Yes. BrowsePad supports tab creation, tab switching, tab closing, and close-all-tabs support.

Can websites request a new window? +

Yes. Sites that try to open a new browser window are handled by opening that content in a new tab.

Does BrowsePad support bookmarks and history? +

Yes. Bookmarks and history each have dedicated internal pages, and BrowsePad also restores previously open non-internal tabs after relaunch.

Can I switch sites into desktop mode? +

Yes. BrowsePad supports per-site switching between Adaptive and Desktop view modes, plus a default view mode setting.

Can I download files? +

Yes. BrowsePad confirms downloads before starting them and uses Android DownloadManager integration where supported.

What if a page fails or has SSL issues? +

BrowsePad blocks insecure certificate flows and shows a friendly internal error page for common failures like timeouts, SSL problems, redirect loops, and host lookup errors.

Where does BrowsePad Connect live? +

BrowsePad Connect is built into the browser as an internal page, including a native TV Connect tab with QR pairing and controller status details.
