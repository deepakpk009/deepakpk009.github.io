BrowsePad Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how BrowsePad ("BrowsePad," "the app," "we," "us," or "our") handles information when you use the BrowsePad web browser application for Android TV and related Android devices.

Short version:

  • BrowsePad is a browser, so websites you visit may collect information about you directly.
  • BrowsePad may store browsing data locally on your device to provide browser features.
  • BrowsePad Connect works over your local network between your phone and TV device and may transmit control actions and text input locally for remote-control features.
  • If you buy or manage a subscription, the relevant app store or billing provider may process purchase and account-related information under its own policies.
  • We do not state that we collect your browsing history, page content, passwords, or form entries on our own servers unless a specific in-app feature clearly says otherwise.
  • You can control much of your data by clearing browsing data, managing permissions, and uninstalling the app.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the BrowsePad Android application. It does not govern the privacy practices of third-party websites, services, streaming providers, search engines, advertisers, or app stores that you may access through BrowsePad.

2. Information Processed On Your Device

To provide normal browser functionality, BrowsePad may process and store information locally on your device, including:

  • browsing history;
  • bookmarks and favorites;
  • open tabs and session-restore data;
  • cookies, cache, and other website storage created by sites you visit;
  • download records and files saved by you to your device;
  • site permissions and browser settings such as desktop mode or search preferences; and
  • local network or pairing data needed for remote-control style features such as BrowsePad Connect, if you choose to use them.

This data is generally used to operate the browser and improve the user experience on your device, not to build a server-side profile about you.

3. BrowsePad Connect And Local-Network Remote Features

BrowsePad includes a local-network remote-control feature called BrowsePad Connect. If you use it, the TV app may generate a QR code or local controller URL so that a phone or other device on the same network can connect to the TV browser.

When connected, the phone controller may send cursor movement, taps, scrolling, zoom gestures, text input, Enter/submit actions, and similar remote-control events to the TV app over your local network, including over a local WebSocket connection. The feature may also keep limited local connection state, such as connection status, recent disconnect information, and device-side diagnostics for connection events or failures.

We do not describe BrowsePad Connect as a cloud remote-control service. Based on the feature list you provided, this feature is designed to work locally between your devices on the same network unless a future version clearly says otherwise.

4. Information We May Receive Automatically

Like most apps and websites, certain technical information may be generated when the app interacts with networks or third-party services, such as IP address, device type, operating system version, app version, crash details, or basic request metadata. Whether that information is received by us depends on the specific services and features enabled in the version of BrowsePad you are using.

If a feature sends data to us or to a third-party service provider on our behalf, that data should be limited to what is reasonably necessary to operate, secure, maintain, or troubleshoot that feature.

5. Information We Do Not Intend To Collect As A Service Provider

Unless a specific feature or in-app disclosure says otherwise, we do not intend to collect or store the following on our own servers:

  • the contents of webpages you browse;
  • your full browsing history;
  • your bookmarks, saved tabs, or downloaded files;
  • passwords, payment card numbers, or form entries; or
  • precise device location.

6. Websites, Search Providers, And Embedded Services

When you visit websites through BrowsePad, those websites and services may collect information directly from your device or browser. This can include your IP address, user agent, cookies, approximate location, pages viewed, search queries, media activity, and information you choose to submit to them.

Those third parties act under their own terms and privacy policies. BrowsePad cannot control how third-party websites or services collect, use, or share your information.

7. Downloads And Files

If you choose to download files through BrowsePad, the app may pass the download request to Android system components such as DownloadManager and may save files to your device’s public Downloads folder or an app-specific external downloads location, depending on the Android version, device capabilities, and permissions available.

Downloaded files are controlled by you and may remain on your device until you delete them. Third-party websites or file hosts may also receive information related to your download requests.

8. Permissions And Sensitive Features

Some websites or browser features may request access to device capabilities such as location, microphone, camera, storage, Bluetooth, nearby devices, or local network access. In general:

  • these permissions are used to provide the feature you requested;
  • website-origin permissions are typically controlled through Android and browser permission prompts; and
  • you can revoke permissions later through your device settings.

If BrowsePad ever uses personal or sensitive data in a way that would not be reasonably expected, that use should be accompanied by an appropriate in-app disclosure and any consent required by applicable law or platform policy.

9. Subscriptions, Purchases, And Store Providers

BrowsePad may offer subscription-related flows through app store providers such as Google Play, Amazon Appstore, or Huawei AppGallery. If you start, renew, restore, verify, or manage a subscription, the relevant store or billing provider may process information such as your store account details, subscription status, transaction identifiers, country or region, billing metadata, and related purchase information under that provider’s own terms and privacy policies.

BrowsePad may locally cache subscription status or recent verification results to support app access and short offline grace-period behavior. We do not describe ourselves as the merchant of record for app-store purchases handled by those providers.

10. How We Use Information

We may use information processed through BrowsePad to:

  • provide browser functionality;
  • remember your settings and session state on your device;
  • support features such as tabs, bookmarks, history, downloads, and remote control;
  • provide local-network pairing, connection, and remote text-input features;
  • support subscriptions, entitlement checks, and store-management entry points;
  • maintain security, prevent misuse, and diagnose technical issues; and
  • comply with legal obligations.

11. Sharing Of Information

We may share information only in limited situations, such as:

  • with service providers acting on our behalf to host, secure, maintain, or troubleshoot app functionality;
  • with store or billing providers when necessary to support purchases, subscription verification, renewals, restores, or account-linked entitlement handling;
  • when required by law, legal process, or a valid government request;
  • to protect rights, safety, security, users, or the public; or
  • as part of a merger, sale, financing, or transfer of some or all of the app or related assets.

We do not state that we sell personal and sensitive user data.

12. Data Retention

Data stored locally in BrowsePad generally remains on your device until you delete it, clear browsing data, remove downloads, clear app storage, or uninstall the app.

If we receive limited operational or support data, we may keep it only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, debugging, or recordkeeping.

13. Your Choices

Depending on the version of BrowsePad and your device, you may be able to:

  • clear browsing history, cookies, cache, and other site data;
  • delete bookmarks, downloads, or saved sessions;
  • disconnect phone-based remote sessions or avoid using BrowsePad Connect;
  • manage site permissions and Android app permissions;
  • disable or avoid optional features you do not want to use; and
  • uninstall the app at any time.

14. Children’s Privacy

BrowsePad is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 as a service provider. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us directly, please contact us so we can review and take appropriate action.

15. Security

We use reasonable measures designed to help protect information under our control. However, no method of transmission, storage, or electronic security is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

BrowsePad is designed to block insecure SSL certificate flows instead of bypassing them, but website security and network conditions outside the app remain outside our control.

16. International Users And Legal Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under applicable privacy laws, such as rights to request access, correction, deletion, or information about how your personal data is handled. You may contact us to make a request, and we will review it in accordance with applicable law.

17. Changes To This Policy
